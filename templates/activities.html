Popular Amenities!

Puerto Rico was home to 4 million tourists in 2020, placing it in the top 50 most popular tourist destinations in the world. Puerto Rico is home to innumerable activities and offers an unforgettable experience. With over 300 beaches, numerous hiking trails, zip-lining, cave exploring, natural waterfalls, bioluminescent bays, nightclubs and immense views, Puerto Rico has everything to offer and more. PUERTO RICO LANDMARKS

Puerto Rico's Rio Camuy Cave Park.

Rio Camuy Cave

Rio Camuy Cave national park is 268 acres large and believed to be the third largest cave system in the world. The caverns are part of a large network of natural caves and underground waterways that are carved out by the third largest underground rivers in the world. Come to a world within a world and explore the what nature has to offer adventurists.

Flamenco Beach aka "Culebra Island".

Flamenco Beach

Flamenco beach is part of a small carribean island called, "Culebra Island". Flamenco beach is famous for it's shallow, turqouise waters, and white sand. The beach is in the shape of a horseshoe and was named the 3rd best beach in the world by TripAdvisor in 2014. Kick off your socks, soak your feet into the white sand and visit this world renowned beach.

Puerto Rico's contemporary art mueseum.

Museo de Art de Puerto Rico

The Museo de Art de Puerto Rico or Puerto Rico's Museum of Contemporary Art is a whopping 130,000sq ft. The museum consists of 24 exhibition galleries all distributed between the historical building's west and east wings. It is said that the museum showcases art dating back to the 17th century. The art museum also hosts a number of events and festivals as well. If you're a fan of the arts, this is surely the place for you.

Puerto Rico's El Yunque national forest.

El Yunque National Forest

El Yunque National Forest is the only tropical rainforest in the United States National Forest System. The forest is located in the Sierra de Luquillo mountains and spans more than 28,000 acres. The highest mountain peak is 3,494 feet above sea level. Some areas of the forest receive up to more than 20 feet of rain per year. Come and explore this vast forest to get lost and become one with nature.
