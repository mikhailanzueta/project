Puerto Rico Landmarks

Puerto Rico is home to numerous historic landmarks
and they all provide tourists with an experience. Here
are a few historic landmarks we recommend you visit:

Castillo San Felipe Fort

Castillo San Felipe Fort better known as "El Morro" is arguably the most famous landmark
on the island. El Morro is located in Old San Juan, the oldest city in the Americas, 500 years old!
El Morro was specifically designed by the Spaniards in the 16th century to protect Old San Juan
from sea attacks. The fort consists of dungeons, barracks, and storerooms. It is said that
the fort has never suffered defeat in its entire history and still has the original cannons.

La Fortazela

La Fortazela or "The Fortress" is the official residence of the governor of Puerto Rico. The fort
was built between 1533 and 1540 to defend the harbor of San Juan. La Fortazela was actually the
first defensive fortification built for the city of San Juan. La Fortazela has been captured by
invader on two occasions, once in 1598 and another in 1625. The fort has been the official residence
of the governor since the 16th century, 1544 to be exact.

Santa Maria Magdalena Cemetery

The Santa Maria Magdalena cemetery is a colonial-era cemetery built outside the walls of
the San Felipe Fort. Construction of the cemetery began in 1863 and was decided to be built
overlooking the ocean. This was done due to the belief in the separation of life and death and
to symbolize the the spirit's journey to cross to the afterlife. The cemetery is grounds to
Puerto Rico's most prominent and native residents throughout it's history.

Castillo San Cristobal Fort

Named after the saint patron of land travelers, Saint Christopher, Castillo San Cristobal fort
is considered the largest fortress in the Americas. The purpose of the fort was to protect San Juan
against attacks from land. The fort consists of three levels and took over 150 years to complete.

Catedral de San Juan Bautista

The San Juan Catholic Cathedral for short, is one of the oldest buildings in San Juan.
It is the oldest church in the United States and the second oldest in the Americas. The original
cathedral was built in 1521, but was destroyed by a hurricane. The current building was constructed
in 1540 and is the model still standing today. The cathedral is said to contain the of tomb
Spanish explorer and settlement founder, Juan Ponce de Leon.
